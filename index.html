Evidence Submission Portal

Document a Comment

Seen a misogynistic, victim-blaming, or abusive comment on a public Facebook post about gender violence? Submit it here. All submissions stay private until reviewed by our editorial team.

Submission Standards
Only submit publicly visible comments from public posts or pages. Do not submit content from private groups, direct messages, or closed conversations. Fabricated or manipulated screenshots will lead to a permanent submission ban.
Contributor Guidelines
What to submit: Publicly visible comments from public Facebook posts or pages that contain misogynistic, victim-blaming, threatening, or dehumanizing language related to incidents of gender violence in Bangladesh.
What NOT to submit: Private messages, content from closed groups, comments unrelated to gender violence, personal disputes, political disagreements that do not involve gender-based abuse, or any content you have fabricated or manipulated.
Screenshot requirements: Screenshots must be unedited, uncropped, and unannotated. They must clearly show the comment text, the commenter's profile name, and the post context. Timestamps should be visible where possible. Do not redact or highlight any portion.
Accuracy expectations: Select the most accurate abuse category and incident type. If unsure, err on the side of caution and add a contextual note explaining your uncertainty. Moderators will reclassify if needed.
Submitter responsibility: By submitting, you confirm the evidence is genuine and unmanipulated. False submissions waste moderator time and undermine the archive's credibility. Repeated false submissions will result in permanent restriction.

About

Why Daag Exists

In Bangla, দাগ means stain, the kind that does not wash off. The name is deliberate. When men post victim-blaming, misogynistic, or violent commentary on public Facebook posts about gender violence, that behavior should leave a record. This project was built in response to a pattern: when incidents of rape, acid violence, or domestic abuse happen in Bangladesh, the comment sections fill with cruelty and then disappear. Daag exists to make sure they do not.

The Problem

Online cruelty
after violence

When news of a rape, acid attack, or domestic violence case spreads on Facebook, the comment sections often become a second site of violence. Victims are blamed for their clothing, behavior, family background, and character. Perpetrators are defended. Survivors are silenced.

These comments disappear quickly. They get moderated, deleted, or buried by newer content. But while they exist, they shape public discourse, discourage survivors from speaking, and signal to perpetrators that their community supports them.

Daag exists to preserve a systematic record of this discourse, not for outrage but for understanding. It is built for researchers, journalists, advocates, and policymakers who need evidence to make the case for change.

Our Approach

How Daag Works

Every entry in the Daag archive goes through a structured review process before publication. Submissions are first screened by AI-assisted tools that check for duplicate content, image authenticity, and URL validity. This automated layer reduces the burden on human reviewers and flags potential issues early.

After initial screening, each submission is reviewed by a human moderator who verifies the source, confirms the comment is from a public post, assesses the category and severity, and ensures the submission meets our editorial standards. Moderators can approve, reject, request additional context, or place submissions on hold for further review.

This combination of AI-assisted screening and human editorial judgment allows us to maintain accuracy at scale while ensuring that every published entry has been reviewed by a person who understands the context. No entry is published without human approval.

Our Commitment

Principles

Transparency
Our methodology, moderation criteria, and data handling practices are documented and publicly accessible. We publish aggregate statistics about submissions, approvals, and appeals so the public can evaluate our work.
Dignity
Even when documenting harmful speech, we design every aspect of this platform to avoid becoming a tool for harassment or mob justice. Archive entries preserve evidence; they do not call for action against individuals. We do not publish private information.
Accuracy
Every submission is verified before publication. We cross-check URLs, validate screenshots, and confirm that archived comments were posted publicly. If an error is discovered after publication, we correct or remove the entry and document the correction.
Accountability
We hold ourselves to the same standard we apply to the content we document. Anyone can file an appeal to challenge an entry. Appeals are reviewed by a separate moderation panel within 14 working days, and outcomes are recorded transparently.

Privacy Policy

This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your information on the Daag platform.

1. Information We Collect

When you use Daag, we may collect the following information depending on how you interact with the platform:

Submissions: Evidence you submit including screenshots, URLs, context notes, category selections, and optional contact email. Contact messages: Your name, email address, subject selection, and message content when you use the contact form. Search queries: Aggregate search patterns may be monitored to detect misuse, but individual queries are not stored or linked to identifiable users. Technical data: Basic request logs (IP address, browser type, timestamps) maintained by our hosting infrastructure for security purposes.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes: to review and moderate evidence submissions; to respond to contact form messages and support requests; to process and resolve appeals; to maintain platform security and prevent abuse; to generate anonymised, aggregate analytics displayed publicly on the platform; and to improve the accuracy and reliability of the archive.

We do not use your information for advertising, marketing to third parties, or any purpose unrelated to the platform's civic accountability mission.

3. Data Retention

Different types of data are retained for different periods:

Contact emails: Messages submitted through the contact form are retained for 180 days, after which they are permanently deleted unless they relate to an ongoing matter. Submissions: Approved submissions are retained permanently as part of the public archive for as long as they remain published. Rejected or withdrawn submissions are deleted within 90 days. Appeals: Appeal records, including supporting evidence and reviewer notes, are retained for 2 years from the date of resolution. Technical logs: Server logs are automatically purged after 30 days.

4. Data Sharing

Your personal information is accessible only to authorised Daag moderators who need it to perform their moderation duties. We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third party.

Approved archive entries (which contain public Facebook comments, not private data) are visible to all platform users by design. Submitter contact information is never displayed publicly. We may disclose information if required by law or to protect the safety of individuals, but we will resist overbroad requests and notify affected users where legally permitted.

5. Screenshots & Evidence

Screenshots submitted as evidence are stored securely and reviewed by our moderation team before publication. All submitted media is verified for authenticity. Screenshots of public Facebook comments are published as part of approved archive entries.

We do not accept or publish screenshots containing private messages, closed-group content, or any material not from publicly visible posts. If a screenshot is found to contain personal information beyond the public comment (such as phone numbers or addresses visible in the background), that information will be redacted before publication.

6. Cookies & Tracking

Daag uses Supabase for backend services, which may set functional cookies necessary for the platform to operate (such as session management). We do not use third-party advertising trackers, social media pixels, or analytics services that track users across websites.

No data is shared with Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any similar third-party tracking service. The aggregate analytics displayed on our platform are computed entirely from our own database and do not involve external tracking.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to: Access any personal data we hold about you; Correction of inaccurate personal information; Deletion of your personal data (such as contact form messages or submitter email addresses); and Objection to processing of your data in certain circumstances.

To exercise any of these rights, please submit a request through our contact page. We will respond to all legitimate requests within 30 days. Note that deletion requests regarding approved archive entries (which document public comments) will be handled through the formal appeals process.

8. Children's Privacy

Daag is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected data from a child under 16, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

If you believe a child has submitted personal information to Daag, please contact us immediately so we can address the situation.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will post a notice on the platform and update the "Last updated" date below.

Continued use of the platform after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, wish to exercise your data rights, or have concerns about how your information is handled, please reach out through our contact page.

We take all privacy inquiries seriously and will respond as promptly as possible.
